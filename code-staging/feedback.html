Assessment and feedback in MATH32032

This page tells you what assessment tasks you will need to complete and when. It also tells you what kind of feedback you will be able to receive. Finally, I touch upon feedback that past students provided to me.

Assessment tasks - for credit

Coursework will consist of two parts to encourage students to engage with the material and to maximise opportunities for feedback before the final exam.

Midterm coursework

Worth 10% of the final mark, this will be a take-home problem sheet posted online on Thursday in Week 05, due on Wednesday in Week 07 at 1pm. There will be a later deadline for those who will submit online - instructions will follow!

Feedback on midterm coursework will likely be provided electronically via individual emails to students during the Easter break - this time should be used for revision.

Online test

Worth 10% of the final mark. You will have to complete this test online, in your own time, between Monday in Week 10 and end of Week 11. You will be able to reattempt the test (with different questions generated every time) if you want to improve your mark.

Feedback quizzes

I give students an opportunity to sit a 10-minute feedback quiz at the beginning of every examples class. The quizzes are not for credit, but individual feedback will be offered.

Students can also get feedback on their understanding directly from the lecturer, for example during the lecturer's office hour.

Online Discussion Forum

The Coding Theory Discussion Forum 2019 is set up on Blackboard and is an excellent way to receive feedback, not only from the lecturer but from other students as well. Many students choose to post anonymously, which is OK. I recommend everyone to subscribe for the forum.

The final exam

This will be a 2-hour paper sat in May or June, worth 80% of the final mark for the course unit. The format and content of the 2019 paper will be similar to the following past papers which you should start attempting no later than during the Easter break.
