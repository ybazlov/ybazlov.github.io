Feedback on UEQ 2018

Tue 05 June 2018

Yuri's response to unit evaluation questionnaires which the students filled in in May 2018

A version of this document was published in the student feedback section of the School of Mathematics website

This was the second year Coding Theory course ran in a new format: it is now being taught in Semester 2. As a result, there are more teaching hours; and I expect the students to be more experienced, compared to Semester 1. After all, for most students taking MATH32032, this is the final semester of their degree course.

The course was taken by slightly more than 150 students. Most students kindly responded to the Unit Evaluation Questionnaire. Judging by the very high scores the course received in the UEQs (see below), the format, the content and the intellectual level of the course were well-pitched. I thank this year’s students for helping to make the course a success.

  • The feedback on my work was helpful: 4.88 out of 5 (agree: 89%, agree or mostly agree: 99%).

    The support classes were helpful: 4.78 out of 5 (agree: 80%, a/ma: 99%)

    Students’ responses praised feedback quizzes held in the tutorials, which helped to monitor the students’ progress and even receive individual feedback by e-mail. Feedback on written coursework was also rated favourably. Criticism:

    • too much coursework for 20% of the final mark: I think that for most students coursework was a very useful means to understand the essential content early on. Only one half of coursework was written, the other half was an online test which many re-took to improve marks, which in itself was a great stimulus to learn and to revise everything.
    • the online quiz was too formulaic and easy: Formulaic – yes, there is a number of simple but essential fomulas to learn and to use; easy – well, only 2 students managed to get full marks from the first attempt!

  • The unit was well organised: 4.93 out of 5 (agree: 93%, a/ma: 100%)

    Criticism: the tablet used for lectures sometimes had to be restarted, wasting some minutes. True (though not much was lost), the tablet is getting old – I’ll try to use the high UEQ scores to persuade the department to buy me a new tablet!

  • The course materials were helpful: 4.90 out of 5 (agree: 90%, a/ma: 100%)

    Students’ responses commended the availability of podcasts, lecture notes and past exam papers. I will continue to provide these materials to students. Criticism: sometimes, problems for the examples class and their solutions were uploaded late – to address this, I will make problems a part of lecture notes (at the end of each chapter) and will streamline the process of releasing the model solutions.

  • The eLearning resources were helpful: 4.85 out of 5 (agree: 88%, a/ma: 98%)

    The Blackboard-based online test was well-received, although see criticism above. I am working on incorporating Maple TA quizzes into coursework; they are more difficult to set up but will hopefully provide more varied opportunities for online testing and feedback.

  • The lecturer’s teaching was excellent: 4.82 out of 5 (agree: 83%, a/ma: 99%)

    I am grateful for excellent feedback on this. No specific issues other than mentioned above, although I was not afraid to be somewhat provocative or controversial – all to encourage students to think and to engage!

  • Overall, I would rate this unit as excellent: 4.75 out of 5 (agree: 78%, a/ma: 98%)

    Thank you. The high satisfaction score encourages me to continue offering intellectual challenge to students and to uphold standards. It is challenging, hard maths we should be – and are – teaching in the final year, and I believe that good results must not be achievable without a genuine effort and hard work.
